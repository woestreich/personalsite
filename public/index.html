Will Oestreich

Behavioral Ecology | Bioacoustics | Oceanography | Conservation Science

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute (MBARI), where I explore the behavior and conservation of migratory animals in dynamic and changing ecosystems.

In this work, I combine approaches from behavioral ecology, bioacoustics, oceanography, and conservation science to better understand (1) how migratory animals make behavioral decisions in the face of environmental variability and change; and (2) how we as humans can more equitably and effectively manage ecosystems to ensure the protection of vulnerable wildlife alongside essential human resource uses.

Previously, I completed a PhD in Biology at Stanford University (2022), BS and MS degrees in Environmental Engineering at Northwestern University (2015), and held positions at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution and the Natural Resources Defense Council.
